Ugali

Cultural Significance: Ugali is a starchy, porridge-like dish made from cornmeal, popular in many East African countries, including Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. It's typically served with stews, vegetables, or meat and is considered a staple food that symbolizes togetherness and tradition.

Cooking Instructions
  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Gradually add 2 cups of maize flour (cornmeal) while stirring continuously.
  3. Reduce heat and stir vigorously until thickened and smooth (about 10 minutes).
  4. Cover and let cook for another 2-3 minutes. Serve warm with stew or vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize flour (cornmeal)
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt (optional)
