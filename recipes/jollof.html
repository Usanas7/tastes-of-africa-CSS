Jollof Rice

Cultural Significance: Jollof Rice is a beloved dish across West Africa, often served during weddings, holidays, and family gatherings. Its origin is widely debated among Nigerians, Ghanaians, and Senegalese, making it a delicious symbol of regional pride.

Cooking Instructions
  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions, then stir in tomato paste and blended tomatoes. Cook until reduced.
  3. Add seasonings: Maggi cubes, thyme, curry powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stir in washed rice and enough water to cook. Cover and simmer on low heat until rice is cooked and fluffy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 2 fresh tomatoes (blended)
  • 2 Scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 Maggi cubes
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil
