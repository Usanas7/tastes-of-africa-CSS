Mandazi

Cultural Significance: Mandazi is a form of fried bread that originated on the Swahili coast. It’s a staple snack among the Swahili people in Kenya and Tanzania. Also known as bofrot or puff-puff in West Africa, Mandazi is loved for its slightly sweet flavor, versatility, and ease of preparation. It’s often enjoyed with tea or served alongside savory dishes.

Cooking Instructions
  1. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, yeast, and warm water to form a dough.
  2. Let the dough rise for about an hour until it doubles in size.
  3. Roll out the dough and cut into triangles.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve warm.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dry yeast
  • ½ cup coconut milk or water
  • Oil for frying
  • Pinch of salt
