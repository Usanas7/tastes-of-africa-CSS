A Brief History of African Cuisine

African cuisine is a reflection of the continent's vast diversity, shaped by geography, climate, and centuries of cultural exchange. From the coastal regions of North Africa to the savannas of Sub-Saharan Africa, each region boasts its own unique ingredients and cooking methods.

Historically, African food has been influenced by both indigenous ingredients and external trade, including Arab, European, and Asian influences. The use of spices, herbs, and locally sourced ingredients like yams, cassava, maize, and plantains play a key role in African culinary traditions.

Today, African cuisine is making a significant impact on the global food scene. Dishes like Jollof Rice, Tagine, and Injera have gained international recognition, and chefs from across the world are embracing African flavors.
